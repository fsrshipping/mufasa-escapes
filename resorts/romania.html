Mufasa Bucegi

Welcome to the Mufasa Resort in Natural Parc Bucegi in Romania. Experience the natural splendor of the Bucegi Mountains at Parcul Natural Bucegi, a land untouched by time for centuries. Go foraging for mushrooms with our local tour guides, or experience traditional Romanian folk music and learn how to make natural crafts. The more adventurous can go hiking through the pristine natural peaks.

Activities

You can enjoy the luxury of our secluded resorts with campfire barbecues or if you're ready for an adventure our safari guides can take you on a mountain trip with our 4x4 tucks. You'll see a variety of wildlife, ranging from mountain goats to wild bears. In the mountain lodge you will be able to drink a beer or some cocktails while enjoying the stunning views.

More about Bucegi

The Bucegi Natural Park was legally established only in the year 2000, with its own administration established three years later. The surface of the park is 32,497 ha and includes, in addition to the Bucegi Massif, other small parts of the neighboring mountain units, such as Leaota. The natural park has more than 15 peaks that exceed 2,000 meters, the highest in the massif being Omu Peak, 2,505 m.
Bucegi Natural Park is a complex habitat that hosts about 3,500 species of animals. There are species such as the Carpathian newt and the yellow-bellied marmot, but also deer, wolf, bear, lynx or capercaillie. In the high area with the rocks you can also find a special bird, the rock butterfly. Black goats can be found in steep areas and on the alpine plateau.

Approximately 35% of the park's surface is occupied by the 14 nature reserves that benefit from a high degree of protection, the most extensive being the Abruptul Prahovean, of approximately 3,500 hectares. Within the strict protection areas there are 46 natural monuments (waterfalls, springs, caves, oats, gorges, towers, fangs, arches, rocks, chimneys, glacial circuses and fossiliferous points), most of geomorphological importance.
One of the best ways to visit the Bucegi National Park is by bicycle. There are over 60 kilometers of bike trails crisscrossing the park, of various lengths and difficulties. Some of them are marked. You can rent a mountain bike from the nearby resorts, and don’t forget to wear a helmet and protective clothing.

Mountain climbing– the Bucegi area has been a hotspot for mountaineers for decades. Some of the most challenging vertical walls in the country are found here, like the Costilei Wall and the Vaii Albe Wall.
A trip to the Babele and the Sphinx is a must-do for any family visiting the Bucegi. The Sphinx, an anthropomorphic formation that resembles the Egyptian Sphinx when looked at from certain angles, is one of the symbols of Romania. Shrouded in legend, the mysterious rock is said to be the place where Dacians carried their sacrifices more than 2,000 years ago. The mushroom-shaped Babele are just as intriguing, albeit less imposing. The simplest way to reach these two formations, located at an altitude of over 2,000 meters is by taking a ride on the cable car from Busteni.
