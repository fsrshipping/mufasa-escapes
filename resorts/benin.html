Mufasa Pendjari

Welcome to the Mufasa Resort in Penjari national park in Benin. In Pendjari you can see a variety of wildlife. Lions, elephants, over 36 different birds, all combined with stunning views. Our drivers are happy to take you on a safari trip through the park to take it all in.
Unpoilt by mass tourism you can enjoy a crowd-free safari. Enjoy the beautiful Tanongou waterfalls, cultural visits to Tata Soma homesteads and Peulh camps. Or you just sit back, relax and enjoy all the luxury our resort has to offer. Besides a pool, campfire and full room service we also offer various workshops given by locals.

The National Park

The Pendjari Biosphere Reserve has a tropical climate of the Sudanese type, characterized by a dry season from the months of October to April, and a rainy season lasting from May to September. The area contains rocky cliffs that are sparsely wooded. The reserve covers a diversity of plant formations such as grassy, shrubby, woodland and forested savannahs, as well as open forests and gallery forests. These formations provide a varied habitat to many species of fauna. Most of the large mammals living in this part of West Africa are to be found in the reserve.
Lions, elephants and buffalos can be easily found, while. leopards are harder to observe. Other species include the cheetah and African wild dog. The reserve is also home to 10 species of antelopes, including the topi and sable antelope, as well as other species such as the hippopotamus. In addition to these mammal populations, the reserve hosts over 460 species of birds. The Nile crocodile is also to be found in the reserve.
